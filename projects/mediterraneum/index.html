Architecture

Mediter-
raneum

Bonn, Germany Study Group Project 2017

A greenhouse typology inserted into an existing courtyard in Bonn — a glass and steel structure that brings Mediterranean climate and vegetation into the heart of the city.

The project explores the greenhouse as an architectural type: a structure defined entirely by its skin, where the frame becomes the facade and the interior is shaped by light, reflections, and the rhythm of the steel grid.

The hexagonal geometry of the floor plan derives from the idea of cellular growth — each module can stand alone or aggregate with others. The steel frame is prefabricated and bolted on site, making the structure both precise and demountable.

Inside, a curated selection of Mediterranean plants creates a microclimate that contrasts with the northern European context. The double-skin glass facade acts as a thermal buffer, collecting solar gain in winter and ventilating passively in summer.

The project was developed as a group work with a focus on construction detail and material precision.
